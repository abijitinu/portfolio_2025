Designing at Scale

Designing a single application that serves diverse user bases across 20+ global markets presented unique challenges. We had to ensure a seamless experience for both B2B professionals and B2C consumers on both iOS and Android platforms.

The complexity of localization, varying device capabilities, and distinct user needs required a robust, scalable design system. We couldn't just design for one ideal scenario; we had to design for flexibility and adaptability at a massive scale.

The Challenge

One of the significant technical challenges in AR visualization is distinguishing between surfaces and boundaries. Without precise control, virtual colours can "bleed" onto adjacent walls or ceilings, breaking the immersion and utility of the tool.

Additionally, the app needed to cater to two distinct user groups: B2C Consumers looking for inspiration and B2B Experts requiring professional-grade accuracy and tools.

The Solution

We redesigned the Visualiser application to be a comprehensive platform for both casual users and professionals. The solution integrates advanced AR technology with practical tools like a tangible colour picker and a vast multicolour collection database.

  • B2C Mode: Focuses on inspiration, allowing users to pick colours from the world, explore products, and save personalised spaces.
  • B2B Expert Mode: Offers precision tools, including tangible device integration for accurate colour matching and project management features like notes and slideshows.

Key Features

1. Precision Masking

To solve the "bleeding" issue, we developed a manual masking feature. Users can define start and finish nodes to create a precise boundary. The system then discards the colour overflow, ensuring a clean edge. We also implemented a "back and forth" editing capability, allowing users to refine their masks at any time without starting over.

2. Tangible Device Integration

For expert users, relying solely on a phone camera isn't enough. We integrated support for a tangible colour picking device. This allows professionals to scan a physical surface and get an exact paint match from the company's database, bridging the gap between physical and digital.

3. Universal UI Research (My Key Contribution)

One of my most significant contributions was the extensive research and design of a "Universal UI" system. The challenge was to accommodate millions of colour variations and diverse colour families (Fan Decks, Hue Bars, etc.) within a single, consistent mobile interface.

I analyzed the structural differences of various colour systems and developed a modular UI framework. This "Universal UI Template" dynamically adapts to the underlying data structure, ensuring that whether a user is browsing a massive fan deck or a curated sub-collection, the navigation remains intuitive and scalable without requiring custom code for each market.

Research & Strategy

Delivering a complex, multi-platform app for 20+ markets within an 8-month timeline required a rigorous and efficient research strategy. We bridged the gap between business goals, customer needs, and Agile development through:

Qualitative Research

Conducted visual ethnographic studies and one-on-one interviews with expert users (Interior Designers, Paint Experts) to understand their specific workflows and conventions.

Quantitative Research

Deployed demographic surveys and questionnaires to a broad base of general consumers to understand colour usage patterns and preferences.

Affinity Mapping

Synthesized research data into clusters to identify key themes and feature requirements, ensuring the app remained feature-rich yet focused.

Priority Matrix

Used a priority matrix to evaluate and rank features for the MVP, balancing innovation with the strict delivery timeline.

Reflections & Learnings

This project was a masterclass in balancing innovation with constraints. Integrating a tangible device with a mobile app was technically demanding, and we had to make tough trade-offs to meet the 8-month deadline.

What went well

The "Universal UI" system proved to be a lifesaver. It allowed us to scale the app to 20+ markets with different product catalogs without rewriting the core interface code.

Challenges faced

The initial masking feature was too complex for casual users. We had to iterate multiple times, simplifying the node interaction logic based on user testing feedback, eventually arriving at the "back and forth" editing model.
