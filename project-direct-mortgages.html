Context & Role

The Background

Direct Mortgages operates as the critical financial heartbeat of the Philip James estate agency network. Covering the North West (Manchester, Cheshire, etc.), they needed to process high volumes of applications while adhering to strict regulatory frameworks (Quilter Financial Planning).

The Brief

As the Lead Designer, I wasn't just asked to "skin" a database; I was tasked with architecting a unified digital command center. The goal was to consolidate fragmented workflows from multiple offices into one scalable platform that professionalized the client journey from viewing to completion.

The Problem: Growing Pains & Data Silos

Before the redesign, the brokerage was struggling with "operational friction." As the team grew across varying locations (Ellesmere Port, Sale, Didsbury), their digital infrastructure couldn't keep up.

We identified three critical friction points through user analysis:

  • Fragmented Truth: There was no single source of truth. Client data lived in silos, making it impossible for management to see a holistic view of the business.
  • Admin Overload: High-value advisors were bogged down by repetitive manual entry (logging prospects, sending routine updates), which detracted from client-facing time.
  • The "Black Box" Effect: Clients and agents often felt in the dark regarding application status, leading to anxiety and unnecessary chase-up calls.

The Solution

A. The "At-a-Glance" Dashboard (Reducing Cognitive Load)

The primary dashboard was designed to answer one question for the broker the moment they log in: "What needs my attention right now?"

Instead of a dense data table, I implemented a widget-based hierarchy:

  • Pipeline Visibility: A real-time funnel (e.g., 180 Active Clients vs. 20 Prospects) immediately informs brokers where they stand against targets.
  • Regional Oversight: A "Clients by Office" widget allows leadership to spot bottlenecks in specific branches (e.g., Tarporley vs. Sale) instantly.
  • Actionable Reminders: We moved away from static to-do lists to "Action Cards" (e.g., "Call HSBC" linked directly to the "Chase Broker Fee" task), reducing the clicks needed to complete a task.

B. The 360-Degree Client Profile (The Single Source of Truth)

To solve the fragmentation issue, we built the "Client 360" view. This is the heart of the system.

I designed this interface to mimic the linear progression of a mortgage application.

  • Visual Status Tracker: An interactive progress bar (0% to 100%) tracks milestones from "DIP" to "Mortgage Offer." This aligns the mental model of the advisor with the actual progress of the application.
  • Compliance Integration: Document management is dangerous if disorganized. I designed a categorized upload system (Proof of Income, ID, etc.) with integrated E-Sign capabilities to ensure the "paper trail" is audit-proof.

C. Standardizing Communication (Brand Consistency)

One of the biggest risks for an in-house broker is inconsistent communication.

  • Template Engine: I designed a system where admins can create pre-approved message templates. This ensures that a "Mortgage Offer" email sent from the Withington office sounds exactly as professional as one from Heaton Moor.
  • The Audit Log: A "Notification Centre" tracks every outbound digital touchpoint. This solves the "he said, she said" problem by creating an immutable history of client engagement.

The Impact

By shifting from disjointed spreadsheets to a User-Centric Dashboard, we achieved:

  1. Operational Velocity: The "Frequent Operations" shortcuts significantly reduced the time-on-task for onboarding new clients.
  2. Scalability: The modular design of the "Office Administration" tools means the system is future-proof. Adding a new branch or advisor is now a standardized 5-minute workflow, not an IT headache.
  3. Client Trust: The transparency provided by the status trackers and professionalized communication tools helps mitigate the pressure-selling stigma often associated with estate agency mortgage brokers.

Design Retrospective

This project reinforced that in complex fintech products, clarity is the most valuable feature. By surfacing the right data at the right time (e.g., the Renewal Reviews list), we didn't just design a tool for storing data; we designed a tool for generating business.
