The Context

Perfit, a leader in industrial solutions, faced a bottleneck in their warehouse operations. Existing systems were disjointed, leading to inefficiencies in inventory tracking and order fulfillment.

The Brief

The goal was to create a unified management portal that could integrate directly with Human-Machine Interfaces (HMI). The system needed to streamline logistics workflows and provide a seamless bridge between manual operators and automated machinery.

The Audience

The primary users are Warehouse Operators and Logistics Managers.

  • Operators: Need large, high-contrast controls for quick interaction, often while wearing gloves.
  • Managers: Need detailed dashboards to monitor throughput and system health.

The Process

We conducted on-site observations to understand the physical constraints of the warehouse environment.

Key insights included:

  • Lighting Conditions: Variable lighting meant standard UI contrast wasn't enough.
  • Physical Ergonomics: Touch targets needed to be significantly larger than standard web apps.

The Solution

We designed a robust management portal that provides real-time visibility into inventory and order status. The interface was optimized for efficiency, allowing operators to interact seamlessly with automated systems through a clean, user-friendly dashboard.

The Outcome

Designing for industrial environments requires a different mindset than consumer apps. Efficiency and error prevention are paramount when users are interacting with physical machinery.

Key Takeaway
