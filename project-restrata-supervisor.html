The Context

"Why did the business decide to tackle this problem?"

Restrata operates in high-stakes industriesâ€”energy, engineering, and critical infrastructureâ€”where knowing the exact location and safety status of every employee is a legal and moral necessity.

Restrataâ€™s ecosystem is anchored by their Employee Safety App, a powerful tool for individual protection. It features verified check-ins (via Face ID), pairing with BLE panic buttons, and "task" tracking to monitor lone workers in real-time. It even uses prohibitive geo-fences to alert users of high-risk zones.

However, a gap existed in the leadership layer. While the app engaged the individual, Supervisors on complex industrial sites (like oil rigs) lacked a dedicated tool to manage this stream of data for mass accountability. They needed to move from manual headcounts to a digital command center that could visualize these real-time signalsâ€”from missed check-ins to geo-fence breaches.

The Brief

"What was the task at hand?"

My task was to design the Supervisor Module for the Restrata ecosystem. While the standard employee app focuses on personal safety (BLE panic buttons, verified check-ins), this Supervisor App needed to handle operational command.

The constraints were significant:

  • Data Density: The app had to display status for hundreds of "People on Board" (POB) without overwhelming the user.
  • Critical Speed: In an emergency (fire, gas leak), every second counts. The "Muster" (evacuation roll call) workflow had to be instant and error-proof.
  • Offline First: Industrial sites often have spotty signals. The design needed to explicitly show when data was cached or live (e.g., "Last Updated 00:00:03 ago").

The Audience

"Who is this for?"

The primary users are Site Supervisors and Safety Officers.

Insight: They are often working in harsh environmentsâ€”bright sunlight, rain, or wearing gloves. They are not sitting at a desk.

Design Implication: The UI needed high contrast, large touch targets, and "glanceable" status updates. They shouldn't have to dig for information; the answer to "Is everyone safe?" must be on the home screen.

The Process

"How did we get here?"

A. The "Truth" Dashboard (POB)

The core value proposition of Restrata is "People Visibility". I designed the home screen to act as a single source of truth.

Solution: A card-based dashboard that instantly breaks down the total count of people on site vs. specific zones (e.g., "Chester Main Zone"). This allows a supervisor to spot anomalies immediatelyâ€”"Why are there 259 people on site but only 5 in the Safe Zone?".

B. Designing for Panic: The Muster Flow

The most critical feature is the "Muster" (emergency assembly).

Challenge: How do we design a "Start Emergency" button that is easy to access but impossible to trigger accidentally?

Solution: I created a guided flow that forces a deliberate choice between "Planned" (Drill) and "Unplanned" (Real Emergency). This small friction point prevents false alarms while ensuring accurate data logging for compliance.

Execution: Once active, the interface shifts to a "Focus Mode." The navigation bar disappears, and the screen becomes a countdown timer and a live casualty counter.

C. Connecting the Physical and Digital

Restrata strives for "Unified Situational Awareness". To bridge the gap between physical badges and digital logs, I designed an Access Control module.

Solution: An NFC scanning interface that allows supervisors to use their mobile device as a card reader. This means a supervisor can "sweep" a muster point and digitally account for people just by tapping their badges, rather than shouting names off a paper list.

D. Managing External Risks (Visitors)

The website highlights "Duty of Care" as a major pillar. Employees are known, but visitors are a variable risk.

Solution: I designed a dedicated Visitor Management flow that tracks "Expected" vs. "On Site" guests. I added "VIP" tags to help supervisors prioritize high-profile guests, ensuring both security and hospitality are maintained.

The Outcome

The Supervisor App successfully extends Restrata's "resilienceOS" into the field. By empowering supervisors with the tools to run digital musters, we reduced the time required to account for personnel during drills.

The design delivers on the company's promise to "protect what matters" by ensuring that even in the chaos of an emergency, supervisors have a clear, calm, and accurate view of their people.
